Random Forest achieved the highest accuracy on three out of four datasets (Insurance: 0.9440, Credit Card: 0.8120, German Credit: 0.7650), while Logistic Regression performed best on the Heart Disease dataset (0.5574). XGBoost showed competitive performance but did not win on any dataset.

F1 scores showed interesting patterns. Random Forest maintained strong F1 scores on Insurance (0.9421) and German Credit (0.8418). However, on the Credit Card dataset, all models showed relatively low F1 scores (0.3555-0.4611) despite reasonable accuracy, suggesting class imbalance issues.

ROC-AUC scores were generally high across most datasets. The Insurance dataset showed the strongest discriminative ability with all three models achieving ROC-AUC above 0.94. The Heart Disease dataset showed unusual behavior where all models achieved similar ROC-AUC (around 0.76) despite large differences in accuracy scores.
